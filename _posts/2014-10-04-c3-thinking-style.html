Phase 0, Week 3

...but my thoughts are telling me yes. (not everyone will get that one). This week at DBC, we are reflecting on our thinking styles as a way to understand how it can be used to our advantage in learning new material yet to come.

Based on the Gregorc Thinking Style, I found something very interesting: I don't readily identify with any one thinking style. In taking the thinking style quiz, I scored evenly across the board as a Concrete Sequential, Abstract Random, and Concrete Random. Great, am I a weirdo? or Does that mean I can be all of these at the same time? I think what this tells me is that I may have different thinking styles depending on the situation at hand. If I were to describe myself, I'd say that I'm adaptable. I think I can take advantage of this at DBC by tuning my thinking style to one or the other depending on the assignment/project and whether a solo challenge, partner, or team challenge. Maybe I can play the best of all worlds?

It's been almost 3 weeks so far in Phase0 at DBC. The biggest struggle for me so far has been organizing all of the information and to-do assignments and gauging how much time it will take. The next biggest struggle has then been juggling the DBC work with the rest of my life. Overall, I do feel a little unorganized and just not knowing where to start (like cleaning up my messy room). Like many things, I say that the hardest part is always the beginning. I don't yet have a vision for the amount of work to expect. My plan is to keep my feelers on and adjust accordingly. Scheduling seems to be more critical now, since I have to schedule everything on my own. I think time-boxing will definitely help in managing my time. At the same time, I want to keep a balance of "getting the work done" vs. really understanding the stuff. I'm not here to just get by, I'm trying to fly. =)

I really really really hope I can maintain a growth mindset. I think it is critical to go in to a program such as DBC to keep an open mind and willing to take on new challenges and not shy away. I am expecting an accelerated program with A LOT of information cramming in a matter of a few months. How to embrace it all? In week 2, learning CSS was brand new to me and it took me a long time to finally understand some of the concepts (and still a work in progress). But I find it fun and continue to find it a rewarding experience in adding more elements to my website. I'm looking forward to doing even more. I hope throughout the program I continue to have the same approach and attitude to the many many more new things to come. It's those general ideas that I will try to implement: Keep an open mind, Take the risk, and Don't be afraid to fail.
